# Data Classification Policy

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes how company data is classified and protected. The goal is to ensure that sensitive information receives the appropriate level of security based on risk and business needs.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:

- All employees
- Contractors and interns
- Third-party service providers handling company data
- All company systems, cloud services, and stored data

## 3. Data Classification Levels

### Confidential
- Highest level of sensitivity.  
- Unauthorized access may cause financial, legal, or reputational harm.  
Examples: customer data, employee information, internal audit results.

### Internal
- Information intended for internal use only.  
- Unauthorized disclosure may have moderate business impact.  
Examples: internal emails, process documents, internal reports.

### Public
- Information approved for external sharing.  
- Minimal business impact if disclosed.  
Examples: marketing materials, public website content, published documents.

## 4. Handling Requirements

### Confidential Data
- Must be encrypted at rest and in transit.  
- Access restricted to authorized roles.  
- Sharing requires security lead approval.  
- Must not be stored on personal devices.

### Internal Data
- Access limited to employees and authorized partners.  
- May be shared internally without additional approval.  
- Encryption recommended when stored in cloud systems.

### Public Data
- No restrictions on distribution.  
- Must be reviewed and approved before publication.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### Security Lead
- Defines classification levels.  
- Reviews requests to reclassify sensitive data.  
- Ensures controls are implemented.

### IT Administrator
- Implements technical controls such as encryption and access enforcement.  
- Maintains audit logs for sensitive data access.

### Employees
- Handle data according to classification level.  
- Report improper handling immediately.

## 6. Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, including access revocation or termination. Serious violations may require legal action.
