# Vendor Management Policy

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes requirements for selecting, evaluating, and monitoring third-party vendors that access company systems, data, or services. The goal is to reduce security, privacy, and operational risks associated with external providers.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:

- All third-party vendors, contractors, and service providers  
- Any product or service that stores, processes, or transmits company data  
- All departments that engage or manage vendors  

## 3. Vendor Risk Categories

### High-Risk Vendors
Vendors that handle confidential data, store critical systems, or process customer information.  
Examples: cloud platforms, payment processors, IT service providers.

### Medium-Risk Vendors
Vendors that support internal operations but do not access confidential data.  
Examples: HR tools, communication platforms.

### Low-Risk Vendors
General-purpose vendors with no access to systems or data.  
Examples: office suppliers, training platforms.

## 4. Vendor Selection Requirements
Before onboarding a new vendor, the requesting department must:

- Identify the purpose of the vendor  
- Assess whether data will be stored, processed, or transmitted  
- Submit a Vendor Risk Assessment to the Security Lead  
- Confirm alignment with business, security, and compliance needs  

## 5. Security Requirements for Vendors
Vendors handling company data must:

- Use encryption at rest and in transit  
- Implement MFA for administrative access  
- Provide breach notification within a reasonable timeframe  
- Maintain secure configurations and patching practices  
- Have their own incident response plan  

Documentation (SOC 2, ISO 27001, or security questionnaire) must be reviewed before approval.

## 6. Contract Requirements
Vendor contracts must include:

- Data protection obligations  
- Confidentiality requirements  
- Breach notification procedures  
- Service Level Agreements (SLAs)  
- Right to audit or request security documentation  

## 7. Monitoring and Review
High-risk vendors must be reviewed:

- Annually for security documentation  
- Annually for performance and SLA compliance  
- After any security incident

Medium- and low-risk vendors must be reviewed as business needs change.

## 8. Offboarding Vendors
When a vendor relationship ends:

- Access to systems must be revoked immediately  
- Company data must be returned or securely destroyed  
- Documentation of the offboarding process must be collected  

## 9. Roles and Responsibilities

### Security Lead
- Performs vendor risk assessments  
- Reviews security documentation  
- Approves or rejects high-risk vendors  

### Compliance Manager
- Ensures vendor contracts include required clauses  
- Tracks annual reviews and vendor status  

### Department Managers
- Identify business requirements for new vendors  
- Ensure proper justification and documentation  

## 10. Enforcement
Failure to follow this policy may result in delays in vendor onboarding, removal of vendor access, contract termination, or disciplinary action for employees who bypass the process.
